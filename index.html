54 Years of World Energy

Climate change is an increasing threat to the survival of the human race, and reducing our carbon footprint has become of paramount importance. A significant battle against climate change consists of changing how the world produces its energy, and which sources – oil, gas, renewables, amongst others – are being utilized. 54 Years of World Energy is a data visualization that aims to help illustrate energy usage for each country in the past 50 years via an interactive map, force-directed circle graph, and bar chart to show energy usage and carbon emissions. This visualization uses the exajoule as our standard unit of measurement. For a frame of reference, the exajoule is equal to one quintillion (1018) joules. The Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami in Japan had 1.41 EJ of energy. Yearly U.S. energy consumption amounts to roughly 94 EJ.
